Health Services and Treatment Options

  • Illness +

    Details about common illnesses such as cold, flu, and other viral infections.

    1. Common Cold

    Cause:

    Caused by viruses like rhinoviruses.

    Symptoms:

    Runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, coughing, sneezing, congestion, slight body aches, mild headache, and low-grade fever.

    Duration:

    Usually lasts 7-10 days.

    Treatment:

    No specific cure; treatment focuses on relieving symptoms. Over-the-counter (OTC) cold medicines, rest, and fluids are recommended.

    Prevention:

    Frequent handwashing, avoiding close contact with infected individuals, and not touching the face with unwashed hands.

    2. Influenza (Flu)

    Cause:

    Caused by influenza viruses (types A, B, C, and D).

    Symptoms:

    High fever, chills, muscle aches, headache, fatigue, weakness, runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, and sometimes gastrointestinal symptoms like nausea and vomiting (more common in children).

    Duration:

    Typically lasts 1-2 weeks, with severe cases lasting longer.

    Treatment:

    Antiviral medications (e.g., Tamiflu), rest, fluids, and OTC pain relievers. Severe cases may require hospitalization.

    Prevention:

    Annual flu vaccination, good hygiene practices, and avoiding close contact with sick individuals.

    3. Viral Infections

    Types:

    Includes a wide range of infections such as gastroenteritis (stomach flu), viral pneumonia, and viral hepatitis.

    Symptoms:

    Vary depending on the virus, but common symptoms include fever, fatigue, muscle aches, cough, and gastrointestinal issues.

    Duration:

    Depends on the type of viral infection; can range from a few days to several weeks.

    Treatment:

    Supportive care, including rest, hydration, and OTC medications to manage symptoms. Antiviral drugs may be prescribed for certain infections.

    Prevention:

    Vaccinations (for preventable viruses), good hygiene, safe food practices, and avoiding contact with infected individuals.

  • Injury +

    Information on handling injuries including fractures, sprains, and other common issues.

    1. Fractures (Broken Bones)

    What is it?

    A fracture is when a bone breaks or cracks.

    Signs:

    • Severe pain
    • Swelling
    • The bone may look out of place
    • Difficulty moving the area

    What to Do:

    • Keep Still: Don’t move the injured area. Use a splint or sling if you have one.
    • Apply Ice: Put ice on the injury to reduce swelling.
    • Get Help: Go to the hospital or call for emergency help.

    Treatment:

    Doctors may put a cast on the bone or do surgery to fix it. You’ll also need rest and physical therapy later.

    2. Sprains

    What is it?

    A sprain happens when you stretch or tear a ligament (the tissue that connects bones in a joint).

    Signs:

    • Pain around the joint
    • Swelling and bruising
    • Difficulty moving the joint

    What to Do:

    Follow the R.I.C.E. method:

    • Rest: Don’t use the injured joint.
    • Ice: Apply ice for 15-20 minutes to reduce swelling.
    • Compress: Wrap the joint with a bandage.
    • Elevate: Keep the injured area raised to reduce swelling.

    Treatment:

    Rest and ice usually help, but severe sprains may need a brace or physical therapy.

    3. Other Common Issues

    Strains

    What is it?

    A strain is when muscles or tendons get overstretched or torn.

    Signs:

    Pain, swelling, and muscle spasms.

    What to Do:

    Rest, apply ice, and compress with a bandage. Elevate the area if possible.

    Dislocations

    What is it?

    A dislocation is when bones in a joint are forced out of place.

    Signs:

    The joint looks out of place, with severe pain and swelling.

    What to Do:

    Don’t try to move it. Get medical help right away.

    Cuts and Scrapes

    What is it?

    These are open wounds that can happen from accidents.

    What to Do:

    • Stop the Bleeding: Press a clean cloth on the cut.
    • Clean the Wound: Rinse with water and apply antiseptic.
    • Cover It: Use a bandage to keep it clean.

    Bruises

    What is it?

    Bruises happen when you get hit, causing blood to collect under the skin.

    What to Do:

    • Apply Ice: To reduce swelling.
    • Rest: Avoid using the bruised area if it hurts.
